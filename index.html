My Journey to Epicodus

Created by Taylor Phillips

I previously worked as a Qualified Mental Health Associate “QMHA” for 10 years at a major non-profit organization that serves youth in Oregon. In order to advance myself further, it would be crucial for me to get more schooling or advanced training.

In late 2016 and 2017, I worked for about 6 months as a Chripractice Assistant, but it ended up being only a job I was able to commit to part-time. On Christmas Day 2016 I returned to the parent company I had previosuly worked for.

This time I was working with older teens in a more reality based program called Sender House. Here I would be assisting and supporting "Residents" with necessary life skills like cooking, cleaning, laundry, job searching, resume building, etc.

My job at the Sender House was stable and a less stressful environment than I previously worked in. That allowed me to get married in 2018 and provided me the opportunity to stay home for 5 weeks, with accrued sick time, after the birth of my son in Spring 2019.

That 5 weeks at home with my family gave me the time I needed to evaluate what my next steps would be and I chose to begin looking into trade work. In Late 2018, I applied and completed testing to become an Apprentice Electrician. This proved to be a long process, and when I was offered a position, it coincided with the birth of my son. It was more important to me that I was home with him and my wife at that time so I chose to turn down the offer.

In November 2019 I attended a workshop at Epicodus in Portland to learn more about the courses offered. I was completely new to the coding and web development field, but I became excited to work with computers and the thought of building digital projects was really appealing. On March 5th, my family and I moved to the Portland area.

I'm amidst my first week at Epicodus and this is a good project to display my skills thus far. Due to current health and safety recommendations, and social distancing practices, I am being quickly brought up to date on how to work remotely.
